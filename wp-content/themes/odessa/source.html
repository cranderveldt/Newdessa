Rotary visitor: Former O-M exchange student

David Egli, a Rotary exchange student at Odessa-Montour High School in the 2008-09 school year and a columnist for this website during that year, paid a visit to the Watkins-Montour Rotary Club Thursday at its weekly luncheon meeting. Egli, who lives in Switzerland and is working in the insurance trade, is on vacation in the U.S. with his girlfriend, Kira Stahel, a music student who specializes in jazz. Egli -- on his second visit here since his exchange year -- told Rotarians he likes "to come back and see how everybody is doing. This is kind of my second home." He and Kira plan to depart for New York City on Tuesday, and then fly home next Friday.

Left: David Egli at the Rotary luncheon

 

'Celebs' to bag groceries to benefit hospital

The Schuyler Health Foundation will hold its annual Celebrity Bag-A-Thon at the Watkins Glen WalMart on Thursday, Aug. 9 from noon to 5 p.m. Prominent figures in the Schuyler County community – including politicians and business people – will bag groceries to benefit Schuyler Hospital. Plus, Schuyler Hospital nurses will be offering free blood pressure checks.

"Celebrities” will include Jim Howell, Linda Confer, Joan Argetsinger, Holly Mosher, Suzanne and Carl Blowers, Wendy Field, and hospital President Andy Manzer (pictured bagging last year).

Walmart will make a donation afterward to the Hospital’s 2012 Family of Friends Campaign. This year’s campaign allows donors to choose where their donation goes – Capital needs, Technology, Equipment, or the Seneca View Skilled Nursing Facility.

 

Racing legend Bill Milliken dies at 101

Racing pioneer and aeronautical and automotive engineer William F. Milliken -- in whose honor a corner on the old Watkins Glen race course was named following a crash he encountered there while competing in the village's inaugural 1948 race -- died Saturday at home near Buffalo at the age of 101.

Milliken, the author of numerous books and articles on engineering -- including an "Engineering Autobiography" titled "Equations of Motion" -- was a key contributor in the planning and design of the Watkins Glen International racetrack

He is survived by his wife, Barbara, son Douglas and daughter Ann. There are no visiting hours, and private services will be held at the family's convenience. Memorials may be made to the International Motor Racing Research Center, 610 S. Decatur St., Watkins Glen 14891.

The Center was the site of celebrations in honor of Milliken on his 99th and 100th birthdays, events he attended. He was also present in Watkins Glen last September for induction into the Legends of the Glen, an honor reserved for a limited number of Watkins Glen racing notables.

Dave Paugh quartet entertains at concert

Bon Ton Roulet riders visit Watkins again

Bicycle riders participating in the annual weeklong Bon Ton Roulet tour through the Finger Lakes arrived in Watkins Glen Wednesday for a two-night stay. A total of 565 cyclists set up tents along and behind the Watkins Glen Elementary and High School complex after peddling down from Penn Yan, their third of five stopovers on the tour, which started and would end in Cortland. Riders from 36 states, Canada and Germany participated, with ages ranging from 7 to 80. The tour went from Watkins Glen -- a destination for most of the Bon Ton Roulet's 16 years -- to Ithaca on Friday, and from there to Cortland Saturday. Watkins was worth a two-night stay, said one official, because "there's lots to do here."

Left: Sign, riders at the school.

Reed talk topics: jobs, drilling, health care

Congressman Tom Reed (left) met with a dozen area residents Saturday in Montour Falls at a town hall meeting. Topics included gas drilling (he's in favor, with proper oversight), health care (he likes parts of President Obama's plan, but would like to see the entire matter approached "piecemeal, to see what works gradually"), and the desirability of paying down some of the national debt and creating jobs. He also said he was "frustrated" that fellow politicians are already in "campaign mode" instead of dealing with key legislative issues.
